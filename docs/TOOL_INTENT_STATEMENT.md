# Tool Intent Statement — Pause.

---

## 1. Core Function

Pause. intercepts the moment between reaching for your phone and losing yourself in it. It guides you through three breaths, asks you to name the impulse, reflects it back without judgment, and then closes — giving you a clear ending and a small sense of accomplishment.

## 2. Human Context

This tool enters the micro-moment of compulsive phone pickup — the 50-150 times per day a person reaches for their device without intention. It addresses the loop of: boredom/anxiety → phone → scroll → guilt → repeat. It does not try to block usage. It creates a speed bump of awareness.

## 3. Intended User

Someone who already knows they check their phone too much. They don't need to be told — they need a gentler alternative to willpower. They want something that respects them, not something that lectures or tracks streaks.

## 4. Behavioral Thesis

Pause. believes that awareness, not restriction, is the first step in behavior change. A single conscious breath between impulse and action can reshape a habit over time. And completing something — even something tiny — creates a sense of agency that endless scrolling never does.

## 5. Dignity Clause

- No shame language. No "you failed" messaging.
- No streaks, no scores, no leaderboards.
- No notifications or reminders.
- No data leaves the device. Everything stays in localStorage.
- The user can erase all their data with one tap.
- The tool never tells the user they have a problem.

## 6. Refusal Clause

This tool will NOT become:

- A social platform
- A gamified habit tracker
- A surveillance tool
- A monetized product with "premium" features
- Something that sends push notifications
- Something that measures or judges screen time

## 7. Atmospheric Tone

Warm. Quiet. Like a candle in a dark room. Not clinical. Not chipper. Not urgent. The typeface is serif. The palette is muted earth tones. The circle breathes. The language is gentle. The experience has a beginning, a middle, and an end — like a small ritual, not an infinite feed.

---

*Interfaces are rituals. Rituals shape behavior. Behavior shapes identity. Design carefully.*
